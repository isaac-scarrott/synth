Reading the trade: A mirrors the pane geometry most literally but pays for it — names truncate hard, and a nested split becomes a mini-treemap that stops reading at a glance by ~3 panes. B keeps the list a scannable vertical column (order, hit-target, hover band all unchanged) and spends only a 2px gutter + a badge to say "these belong to one split"; orientation lives in the badge glyph, not the layout. C is the middle — an explicit boxed object that names the split and carries a true geometry glyph, at the cost of a heavier container and an indent shift. All three keep the active pane on the existing .session--open accent (002).
